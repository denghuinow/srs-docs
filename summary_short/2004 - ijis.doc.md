# Short Summary

- **Background and objectives**: The Tarrant County Integrated Justice Information System (IJIS) aims to enhance justice administration by improving information sharing and management capabilities. This phase focuses on defining business requirements for managing indigent defense services to ensure effective and timely legal representation for defendants.

- **In scope**:
  - Submit and process requests for appointed counsel.
  - Manage defendant contact and attorney notifications.
  - Handle defense attorney compensation and appointment lists.
  - Generate compliance reports for the Texas Fair Defense Act.
  - Integrate with external systems like Court Case Management.

- **Out of scope**:
  - Expanding beyond criminal justice stakeholder processes.
  - Non-indigent defense legal services.
  - External law enforcement investigative workflows.
  - Detailed financial system implementations outside SAP.
  - Non-Tarrant County jurisdictional procedures.

- **Roles and core use cases**:
  - As a Magistrate, I want to determine defendant indigency and submit counsel requests so that eligible defendants receive timely legal representation.
  - As a Defense Attorney, I want to report initial defendant contact and services performed so that compliance and compensation are accurately tracked.
  - As the Office of Attorney Appointments, I want to manage appointment lists and generate reports so that county indigent defense services meet state standards.

- **Success metrics**:
  - Process counsel requests within one working day of receipt.
  - Notify appointed attorneys and defendants of appointments within 24 hours.
  - Capture and report first defendant contact within one working day of appointment.

- **Major constraints**:
  - Must comply with Texas Code of Criminal Procedure and Government Code.
  - Integration with existing Court Case Management and financial systems.
  - Adherence to Texas electronic transaction and record management guidelines.
  - Support for capital case and death penalty appointment procedures.
  - Reporting aligned with Task Force on Indigent Defense requirements.

- **Undecided issues**:
  - Not mentioned.
