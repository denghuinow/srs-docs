# Balanced Summary

- **Goals and scope**  
The system aims to manage indigent defense services for Tarrant County by streamlining appointment of counsel, tracking attorney activities, and ensuring compliance with the Texas Fair Defense Act. It facilitates information sharing across the criminal justice community while improving efficiency in administering justice. The scope includes processes from initial defendant request through attorney compensation and reporting.

- **Roles and user stories**  
  - As a Magistrate, I want to submit a Request for Appointed Counsel so that eligible defendants receive legal representation.  
  - As a Defense Attorney, I want to log defendant contact details so that compliance with appointment timelines is recorded.  
  - As the Office of Attorney Appointments, I want to process appointment requests so that attorneys are assigned promptly.  
  - As a Court Coordinator, I want to enter setting information so that hearings are scheduled and tracked.  
  - As a Judge, I want to review attorney payment requests so that compensation is approved according to guidelines.

- **Key processes**  
  1. Trigger: Defendant arrested. Detention Officer captures defendant and financial information.  
  2. Trigger: Indigency hearing. Magistrate determines indigency and submits counsel request.  
  3. Trigger: Request received. Office of Attorney Appointments processes request and assigns attorney from rotation list.  
  4. Trigger: Attorney appointed. Defense Attorney records initial defendant contact.  
  5. Trigger: Charges filed. District Attorney notifies system of accepted/rejected charges.  
  6. Trigger: Hearing scheduled. Court Coordinator notifies parties and records outcomes.  
  7. Trigger: Payment request submitted. Judge reviews and approves attorney compensation.

- **Domain data elements**  
  - Defendant: Defendant ID, Financial Affidavit, Contact Information, Indigency Status, Arrest Details  
  - Defense Attorney: Attorney ID, Qualifications, Appointment List Status, Contact Log, Payment Requests  
  - Case: Case ID, Charges, Filing Decision, Court Settings, Disposition  
  - Appointment Request: Request ID, Submission Date, Indigency Determination, Attorney Assigned  
  - Payment: Payment ID, Services Performed, Approved Amount, Judge Approval  
  - Report: Report Type, Date Range, Metrics, Distribution List

- **Non-functional requirements**  
  - Integration with Court Case Management System  
  - Compliance with Texas Code of Criminal Procedure  
  - Support for electronic transactions per Texas UETA  
  - Adherence to Texas DIR guidelines for electronic records  
  - Reporting capabilities for Texas Fair Defense Act metrics  
  - Data exchange alignment with Global Justice XML Data Model (GJXDM)

- **Milestones and external dependencies**  
  - Finalize business requirements through community workshops  
  - Integration with external Court Case Management System  
  - Compliance with Tarrant County Indigent Defense Plan  
  - Dependence on Texas state legal and technical standards  
  - Coordination with Task Force on Indigent Defense reporting

- **Risks and mitigation strategies**  
  - Legal non-compliance: Implement strict adherence to CCP articles and county plans  
  - System integration failures: Define clear interfaces with external justice systems  
  - Data inconsistency: Establish validation rules and audit trails  
  - Timeline delays: Use iterative review cycles with stakeholder workshops  
  - Attorney qualification issues: Maintain updated rotation lists with judicial oversight
