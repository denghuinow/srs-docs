# 2004 - ijis.md

## System Overview
The Integrated Justice Information System (IJIS) for Tarrant County aims to enhance the administration of justice by facilitating rapid information sharing across the Criminal Justice Community and providing advanced justice management capabilities.^[1]^

## Key Requirements

### Business Use Cases
- **Submit Request for Appointed Counsel**: 
  - Enable law enforcement agencies to capture defendant and incident information within 48 hours of arrest.^[2]^
  - Allow magistrates to electronically review and submit requests for counsel within 24 hours of the indigency hearing.^[3]^
- **Process Request for Appointed Counsel**: 
  - Enable the Office of Attorney Appointments to review and process requests for counsel by the end of the first working day.^[4]^
  - Notify law enforcement agencies, appointed attorneys, and defendants of request outcomes within 24 hours.^[5]^
- **Manage Defendant Contact**: 
  - Enable appointed attorneys to capture the date and type of first contact and first in-person visit with the defendant within one working day.^[6]^
  - Notify the Office of Attorney Appointments if first contact is not made.^[7]^
- **Manage Notice of Case Filing Decision**: 
  - Notify appointed attorneys and the Office of Attorney Appointments of the District Attorney's decision to proceed with prosecution and any charge modifications.^[8]^
- **Manage Defense Attorney Setting Notification**: 
  - Notify defendants, defense attorneys, and District Attorneys of court settings.^[9]^
  - Record and update setting information and outcomes.^[10]^
- **Manage Defense Attorney Compensation**: 
  - Enable defense attorneys to submit payment requests.^[11]^
  - Facilitate payment approval, processing, and notification.^[12]^
- **Manage Attorney List**: 
  - Manage the rotation list of defense attorneys for appointments.
  - Handle attorney applications, approvals, removals, and reinstatements.^[13]^
- **Manage Reporting**: 
  - Generate comprehensive reports reflecting compliance with the Texas Fair Defense Act.^[14]^
  - Provide detailed reporting on appointment metrics, case filings, and dispositions.^[15]^

### Legal and Technical Requirements
- **Legal Requirements**: 
  - Comply with the Texas Code of Criminal Procedure (CCP) and Texas Government Code.^[16]^
  - Adhere to the Uniform Electronic Transactions Act (UETA) and Local Government Code.^[17]^
- **Technical Requirements**: 
  - Integrate with existing Court Case Management System and District Attorney Electronic Case Filing System.^[18]^
  - Follow Texas Department of Information Resources guidelines for electronic transactions and signed records.^[19]^
  - Utilize the Global Justice XML Data Model (GJXDM) for data exchange.
