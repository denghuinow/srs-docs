# 2007 - nlm.md

## System Overview
The NLM Digital Repository is designed to manage and preserve digital content in various formats, ensuring long-term accessibility.^[1]^ It supports ingestion, storage, preservation, and access of digital materials for NLM staff and the general public.

## Key Requirements

### Functional Requirements
- **Ingest**: Accept SIPs from producers, perform QA, generate AIPs, and coordinate updates.^[2]^
- **Archival Storage**: Store AIPs, manage storage hierarchy, refresh media, and perform error checking.^[3]^
- **Data Management**: Administer database, perform queries, generate reports, and handle database updates.^[4]^
- **Access**: Provide user access controls, rights management, search and retrieval functionality, and generate DIPs.^[5]^
- **Preservation Planning**: Monitor technology and user community, develop preservation strategies and migration plans.^[6]^

### Policies
- **Collection Development**: Follow NLM's policies for material selection and digitization criteria.^[7]^
- **Security**: Adhere to HHS/NIH/OCCS security policies for system and data security.
- **Metadata**: Use Dublin Core, MARC21, and other approved metadata schemas.^[8]^
- **Ingest Policies**: Define submission agreements, content quality, and format requirements.

### Technical Requirements
- **Standards Compliance**: Adhere to international best practices and standards such as OAIS, TEI, and Z39.50.
- **Format Support**: Support multiple file formats including PDF, JPEG, TIFF, XML, etc.^[9]^
- **System Architecture**: Ensure minimal duplicate data entry, support for multiple languages, and distributed custodial models.^[10]^
- **Accessibility**: Provide both onsite and offsite access, supporting Section 508 compliance.^[11]^
