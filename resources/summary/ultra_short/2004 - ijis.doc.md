**Purpose & Scope**  
Manages court-appointed defense services for indigent defendants in Tarrant County, Texas. Covers request submission, appointment processing, attorney contact tracking, case filing coordination, and compensation. Excludes financial management beyond attorney payments and does not handle case disposition or legal representation decisions.

**Product Background / Positioning**  
Part of Tarrant County’s Integrated Justice Information System (IJIS) initiative. Integrates with Court Case Management System and District Attorney Electronic Case Filing System to enable shared justice data flow. Replaces manual processes for indigent defense coordination across county agencies.

**Core Functional Overview**  
1. Submit and process requests for court-appointed counsel within 24 hours.  
2. Manage defendant-attorney contact tracking and first-visit reporting.  
3. Coordinate case filing decisions with District Attorney.  
4. Schedule and manage court settings for defendants.  
5. Process attorney compensation requests and approvals.  
6. Maintain attorney appointment rotation lists.  
7. Generate compliance reports for Texas Fair Defense Act.  

**Key Users & Usage Scenarios**  
- **Office of Attorney Appointments**: Manages all counsel requests, appointments, and compensation.  
- **Defense Attorneys**: Submit contact logs, request payments, and update appointment lists.  
- **Magistrates/Court Coordinators**: Submit requests, set court dates, and confirm defendant status.  
- **District Attorney**: Files charges and notifies of case decisions.  
*Scenario*: Magistrate submits indigency request → Office processes within 24 hours → Attorney contacts defendant within 1 working day → Compensation processed after case resolution.

**Major External Interfaces**  
- Court Case Management System (for case data and custody status).  
- District Attorney Electronic Case Filing System (for charge filings and decisions).  
- External accounting systems (for compensation payments).  

**Key Non-functional Requirements**  
- Requests processed within 24 hours of submission.  
- First attorney contact confirmed within 1 working day of appointment.  
- Compensation approvals completed within 24 hours of request.  
- Reporting generated per Texas Fair Defense Act compliance metrics.  
- All electronic transactions comply with Texas UETA and DITR guidelines.  

**Constraints, Assumptions & Dependencies**  
- Must comply with Texas Code of Criminal Procedure Articles 1.051, 15.17, 26.04–26.052.  
- Requires integration with Court Case Management System and DA Electronic Case Filing System.  
- Depends on existing Court Case Management System for case data accuracy.  

**Priorities & Acceptance Approach**  
Top priority: Legal compliance (Texas Fair Defense Act and CCP requirements).  
Acceptance criteria: All 24-hour processing windows met; no legal violations in reporting; successful integration with Court Case Management System.
